Where History Comes Alive

Nestled in the heart of Karnataka, Bidar is a treasure trove of architectural brilliance and cultural heritage. Once the capital of the mighty Bahmani Sultanate, this historic city stands as a testament to centuries of artistic excellence, religious harmony, and architectural innovation. From majestic forts to serene gurudwaras, from ancient madrasas to mystical springs, Bidar invites you to step back in time and experience the grandeur of medieval India.

Bidar Fort

The Magnificent Citadel of the Deccan

Standing majestically since the 15th century, Bidar Fort is an architectural masterpiece that showcases the perfect blend of Persian, Arabic, and Indian design elements. Spread across 5.5 kilometers, this formidable fortress features triple moat systems, 37 bastions, and stunning monuments including the Rangin Mahal (Colored Palace) with its intricate tile work and mother-of-pearl inlays.

Bahmani Tombs

Royal Mausoleums of Eternal Grandeur

The Bahmani Tombs at Ashtur are architectural marvels that house the remains of the Bahmani sultans and their families. These stunning Indo-Islamic structures feature massive domes, intricate calligraphy, and beautiful Persian-style paintings. The tombs stand in a serene complex, each telling stories of the dynasty that once ruled the Deccan with magnificent splendor.

Gurudwara Nanak Jhira Sahib

The Miraculous Spring of Guru Nanak

A sacred Sikh pilgrimage site where Guru Nanak Dev Ji is believed to have created a freshwater spring through a miracle. The serene gurudwara attracts thousands of devotees annually who come to experience its spiritual ambiance and witness the eternal spring that flows from the rock. The site beautifully represents the message of universal brotherhood and divine compassion.

Mahmud Gawan Madrasa

Ancient Seat of Islamic Learning

Built in 1472 by Mahmud Gawan, the prime minister of the Bahmani Kingdom, this madrasa was once a renowned center of learning that attracted scholars from across the Islamic world. Despite being partially destroyed by a lightning strike, the remaining structure still showcases stunning calligraphy, intricate tile work, and three-story architecture that speaks of medieval educational excellence.

Narasimha Jhira Cave Temple

Mystical Underground Sanctuary

A unique cave temple carved deep into the rocky hillside, housing a magnificent idol of Lord Narasimha. Devotees must wade through knee-deep water in a 300-meter-long cave passage to reach the sanctum, making it a truly adventurous spiritual journey. The temple's mysterious setting and the perpetual spring water create an otherworldly atmosphere that captivates every visitor.
